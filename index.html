Team LunarBot

We are a group of students competed in Singapore Space Challenge 2021. We are designing a rover model capable of carrying out excavation and/or In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) missions on the moon.

Lunar Bot is the team participating in Singapore Space Challenge 2021 representing Sri Lanka. Our goal is to develop a Lunar Rover design capable of carrying out excavation and/or In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU) missions on the moon. The Rover should be less than 1m3 in size and 60kg in weight.

We followed a unique design approach to overcome this challenge. The self adapting wheel and the Autonomous Extendable Drill that we designed makes our rover outstanding from other competitors.

From concept to design...

The Autonomous Extendable Drill

SWORD (Selenography and Water Observatory Regolith Drill) is a drilling system, which can automatically adjust the length of the drill bit. Can be used to store and extend a long drill in a compact space.

The Self Adapting Wheel

This has the capability of adjusting the grouser heights as required in real time to minimize sinkage. This can exponentially minimize the risk of sinkage and minimizes a few limitations such as inability to adjust payload weight of a rover.
